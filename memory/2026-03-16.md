## Fixed TP vs Trailing Stop Research (7:14-7:30 AM MST)

**Question:** Would broker-level fixed TP be more consistent than trailing stop?

**Research:** Simulated 225 trades using actual 1-min bar high/low data

### Results

| Strategy | Total P&L | WR% | Avg Win | Avg Loss | Bust Rate |
|----------|-----------|-----|---------|----------|-----------|
| **Trailing Stop** | **$25,220** | 64% | $360 | -$345 | **0%** |
| Fixed TP 5pt | $16,200 | 96% | $200 | -$3,000 | 4% |

### Key Findings

**Trailing stop makes 55% MORE money** but lower consistency:
- Captures big moves (avg win $360 vs $200)
- Smaller avg loss (-$345 vs -$3,000)
- NEVER busts funded account (all losses < $2,000 EOD DD limit)

**Fixed TP is more consistent** but caps upside:
- 96% WR feels safer
- Every win is exactly $200
- BUT: When it loses, hits hard SL (-$3,000) = instant account bust
- 9 busts in 225 trades (4% bust rate)

### Funded Account Economics

**Trailing Stop:**
- Never busts (avg loss -$345 < $2,000 limit)
- Runs all 225 trades → $25,220 profit
- **$111.71/day, $28,151/year**

**Fixed TP 5pt:**
- Busts every 25 trades (avg)
- Each bust = $85 + 14 days restart
- Net: $1,715 profit per 39 days
- **$43.97/day, $11,082/year**

**Verdict:** Trailing stop wins by $17,070/year (+154%) when accounting for bust economics.

**Today's loss context:** 40pt/min velocity bar (8× normal). Trail stop logic worked correctly. Market order filled at bar close after massive whipsaw. With fixed TP 5pt, would have exited at +$200 before the crash. But over 225 trades, trailing stop still dominates.
